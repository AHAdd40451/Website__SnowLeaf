Visitor Visa Overview

The Visitor Visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is a travel document that allows individuals to visit Canada for a limited period of time for purposes such as tourism, visiting family or friends, or attending business meetings. It is required for citizens of certain countries to enter Canada and is issued by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). To apply for a Visitor Visa, applicants must provide various documents to demonstrate their eligibility and intention to leave Canada at the end of their authorized stay. The length of stay allowed with a Visitor Visa varies, but typically ranges from six months to two years.
